Internal Medicine

Internal medicine is a medical specialty focused on the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of diseases in adults. Physicians who specialize in internal medicine are called internists. They are trained to manage a wide range of medical conditions affecting various organ systems in the body

Internists provide comprehensive care for adults, including

Preventive care

Internists emphasize preventive measures such as vaccinations, health screenings, and lifestyle modifications to reduce the risk of developing chronic diseases.

Health promotion and education

Internists emphasize preventive measures such as vaccinations, health screenings, and lifestyle modifications to reduce the risk of developing chronic diseases.

Coordination of care

Internists emphasize preventive measures such as vaccinations, health screenings, and lifestyle modifications to reduce the risk of developing chronic diseases.

Diagnosis and treatment

Internists are skilled in diagnosing and managing complex medical conditions, including chronic diseases such as diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, and autoimmune disorders.

Acute care:

Internists are trained to manage acute illnesses and medical emergencies that require hospitalization or immediate attention.
