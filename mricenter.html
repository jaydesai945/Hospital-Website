Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is a state-of-the-art diagnostic imaging technique that offers unparalleled insight into the body's internal structures. By utilizing powerful magnets and radio waves, MRI creates highly detailed images of organs, tissues, and bones without exposing patients to ionizing radiation. During an MRI scan, patients lie comfortably on a table that slides into a cylindrical machine housing the MRI scanner.. As the scanner generates a strong magnetic field and emits radio waves, hydrogen atoms in the body align and emit signals that are captured by detectors.
